Kristen Del Dosso is the founder and CEO of Anti-na®, a woman-owned wellness brand based in Irvine, California. A CPA and MBA by training, Kristen built Anti-na after watching her mother and close family friends navigate chemotherapy — and noticing that raw ginger burned their sensitive mouths while ginger ale had almost no active ingredient. She set out to fill the gap.

Anti-na® SIPS, her flagship product, combines 2,000mg therapeutic ginger root extract with 1,000mg Bioenergy Ribose® in a dissolvable stick pack that addresses both chemotherapy-induced nausea and the cellular fatigue that follows treatment. The product is now recommended by 283 independent clinicians through FrontrowMD and has been featured in Wellness Master Club and the Growing to Give Cancer Recovery Gift Guide. Through the SIPS for Support program, every purchase donates one day of SIPS to a Southern California cancer patient in need.
Full founder story
Kristen Del Dosso is a CPA, MBA, and the founder of Anti-na® — a woman-owned wellness company based in Irvine, California, built on a simple promise: that people navigating treatment deserve to feel present for the moments that matter.

Kristen is not a cancer survivor. But she has sat in that waiting room. She watched her mother go through chemotherapy and saw her choose between the burning pain of raw ginger and the crushing weight of nausea — because nothing in between existed. She watched other women go back to work the day after infusion appointments, not because they wanted to, but because they had to. And she saw that nothing was actually helping them get through those days.

As a hiker and business owner, Kristen had also experienced altitude nausea on Mt. Whitney in 2018 — the moment she first asked herself: "There has to be a better way." She spent the next two years developing what became Anti-na® SIPS: a dissolvable stick pack combining 2,000mg of therapeutic-dose ginger root extract (the dose validated in clinical research on CINV) with 1,000mg of Bioenergy Ribose® to address the cellular energy depletion that chemotherapy causes.

SIPS started as a promise to her mother. It became a promise to every patient who deserves relief that respects what their body is going through. To date, over 400 packs have been donated to Southern California cancer patients through the SIPS for Support program, in partnership with Cancer Kinship and The Breast Cancer Fundraiser.
